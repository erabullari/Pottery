Some History

► Early Pottery: The earliest known pots were made by hunter-gatherer societies. These early pots were typically made by coiling clay into shape and then firing them in open fires or simple kilns. They were often used for cooking food and storing water.

► Neolithic Revolution: With the advent of agriculture around 10,000 BCE, pottery became more widespread and sophisticated. The invention of the potter's wheel around 3,000 BCE revolutionized pottery making, allowing for the production of more uniform and intricate designs.

► Ancient Civilizations: Pottery played a significant role in the development of ancient civilizations. In Mesopotamia, Egypt, China, and other early cultures, pottery was used for everyday purposes such as cooking, storage, and religious ceremonies.

► Greek and Roman Pottery: The ancient Greeks and Romans made significant advancements in pottery making, producing pottery that was not only functional but also highly artistic. Greek pottery, known for its black-figure and red-figure styles, often depicted scenes from mythology and daily life.
